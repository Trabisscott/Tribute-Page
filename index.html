VIRAT KOHLI

INDIA's GREAT

Virat Kohli born 5 November 1988 is an Indian international cricketer and the former captain of the Indian national cricket team. He currently represents Royal Challengers Bangalore in the IPL and Delhi in domestic cricket. Considered to be one of the best cricketers in the world, he is widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in the history of the sport. Nicknamed "The King", due to his dominant style of play and popularity, Kohli holds numerous records in his career across all formats. In 2020, the International Cricket Council named him the male cricketer of the decade. Kohli has also contributed to India's successes, captaining the team from 2014 to 2022, and winning the 2011 World Cup and the 2013 Champions trophy. He is among the only four Indian cricketers who have played over 500 matches for India. Born and raised in New Delhi, Kohli trained at the West Delhi Cricket Academy and started his youth career with the Delhi Under-15 team. He made his international debut in 2008 and quickly became a key player in the ODI team and later made his Test debut in 2011. In 2013, Kohli reached the number one spot in the ICC rankings for ODI batsmen for the first time. During 2014 T20 World Cup, he set a record for the most runs scored in the tournament. In 2018, he achieved yet another milestone, becoming the world's top-ranked Test batsman, making him the only Indian cricketer to hold the number one spot in all three formats of the game. His form continued in 2019, when he became the first player to score 20,000 international runs in a single decade. In 2021, Kohli made the decision to step down as the captain of the Indian national team for T20Is, following the T20 World Cup and in early 2022 he stepped down as the captain of the Test team as well.

Awards

Virat Kohli, the renowned Indian cricketer, has received numerous awards and honors throughout his career, both for his cricketing achievements and his contributions to the sport. Some of the most notable awards and accolades he has received include: 1. Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy: Kohli has been awarded the Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for the ICC Cricketer of the Year multiple times, recognizing him as the best cricketer in the world. 2. Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna: He received India's highest sporting honor, the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna, in 2018, for his exceptional contributions to cricket. 3. Arjuna Award: Kohli was honored with the Arjuna Award in 2013, another prestigious Indian sports award, in recognition of his outstanding achievements in cricket. 4. Padma Shri: He was awarded the Padma Shri, one of India's highest civilian awards, in 2017, for his exceptional contribution to the sport of cricket. 5. ICC Cricketer of the Year: Kohli has consistently been named the ICC Cricketer of the Year for his exceptional performances and consistent run-scoring in international cricket. 6. Wisden Cricketer of the Year: He was named one of Wisden's Cricketers of the Year, a highly respected recognition in the world of cricket. 7. BCCI Polly Umrigar Award: Kohli has received this prestigious award for being the best international cricketer multiple times. 8. ICC ODI Cricketer of the Year: He has been honored as the ICC ODI Cricketer of the Year multiple times in recognition of his outstanding performances in One Day Internationals. 9. ICC Test Cricketer of the Year: Kohli has been named the ICC Test Cricketer of the Year in recognition of his impressive performances in Test cricket. These awards and honors showcase Virat Kohli's incredible talent and contributions to the sport of cricket and his status as one of the finest cricketers in the world.
